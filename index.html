🏠 Home

Who We Are: Curious Quarks is a nationwide network of physics undergraduates from premier universities and research institutes. We collaborate on accessible review articles, host seminars, and create learning materials to bridge classroom theory with hands-on research experience.

Our Vision: To create a vibrant platform where students gain hands-on research experience, explore new ideas, and prepare for higher education and professional roles in physics, data analysis, and scientific communication.

Get Involved: Join us for collaborative writing sprints, reading groups, and mentorship programs. Whether you're new to research or an experienced contributor, we provide opportunities to learn, present, and publish.

🎯 Objectives

Curious Quarks aims to bring together physics undergraduates across the nation to explore, innovate, and collaborate. Through research projects, seminars, and workshops, we provide mentorship, resources, and a forum for students to publish collaborative reviews and project reports.

Our short-term objectives include running regular literature review workshops, pairing students with faculty mentors, and organizing inter-college seminars. Long-term, we aim to build an open repository of student-led review articles and project documentation.

🚀 Our Activities

  • 📝
    Collaborative Research Working on review articles and literature summaries across diverse physics topics — organized as short, focused groups.
  • 📢
    Event Updates Sharing information about inter-college seminars, workshops, and student-run webinars to broaden learning opportunities.
  • 🤝
